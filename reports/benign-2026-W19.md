## I. Immune Thrombocytopenia (ITP)

**CDK8/CDK19 inhibition restores Treg homeostasis and ameliorates thrombocytopenia in murine ITP.**
Wang Y et al. *Blood* 2026-04-30. https://doi.org/10.1182/blood.2025031332

Small-molecule inhibitor AS2863619 (AS) selectively inhibited CDK8/CDK19, converting CD4+CD25− effector T cells into CD4+CD25+Foxp3+ regulatory T cells (Tregs) with lineage stability and suppressive capacity. Mechanism: AS rapidly augmented STAT5 phosphorylation (Foxp3 induction) and suppressed STAT3 phosphorylation under IL-6-driven conditions, attenuating Th17 polarization. Global transcriptomics confirmed a profound shift from Teff to Treg gene programs. Functional metabolic analysis showed glycolysis suppression facilitating Treg conversion. In an active ITP murine model, CDK8/CDK19 inhibition elevated Treg frequencies and **ameliorated thrombocytopenia in a STAT5-dependent manner**. This identifies CDK8/CDK19 as a novel immunomodulatory target in ITP.

**Sex-stratified proteomics and metabolomics predict chronicity in pediatric ITP.**
Xue Y et al. *BJH* 2026-05-03. https://doi.org/10.1111/bjh.70521

Prospective observational cohort; 67 newly diagnosed pediatric ITP patients + 40 healthy controls; minimum 1-year follow-up. Sex-specific predictive models constructed:

- **Males**: ICAM-1 (protein) and biopterin (metabolite) associated with chronic progression
- **Females**: ACTA-2 (actin, alpha 2, smooth muscle) and N6-acetyl-L-lysine associated with chronic progression
- Cross-sex application of biomarkers yielded limited predictive value, confirming sex-specificity

Predictive efficacy confirmed by ROC, calibration, and clinical decision curves. These findings suggest sex-stratified omics profiling at diagnosis could guide early therapeutic decisions in pediatric ITP (watch-and-wait vs early treatment).

**HLA alleles influence susceptibility and prognosis in pediatric ITP.**
González de Pablo J et al. *BJH* 2026-04-29. https://doi.org/10.1111/bjh.70483

Retrospective study; 135 pediatric primary ITP patients vs 929 healthy controls. Median age 5.71 years (IQR 2.71–10.21). Key findings:

| HLA Allele | Association |
|---|---|
| HLA-DRB1*07, HLA-DRB1*13 | Protective (more frequent in controls) |
| HLA-A*68, HLA-B*40, HLA-DRB1*14 | Increased ITP susceptibility (P<0.05) |
| HLA-A*26 | Lower recovery rate |
| HLA-C*03 | Higher recovery likelihood |
| HLA-A*02, HLA-C*04 | Higher chronicity probability |

Results support HLA typing as a potential diagnostic and prognostic tool in pediatric ITP, though validation in larger cohorts required.

**Thrombocytopenia differential: ITP vs hematologic malignancy — critical review.**
Vijenthira A et al. *BJH* 2026-04-24. https://doi.org/10.1111/bjh.70498

Focused review on the overlap between isolated thrombocytopenia, ITP misclassification, clonality-autoimmunity interactions, and downstream malignancy risk. Key message: isolated thrombocytopenia diagnosed as ITP may mask underlying clonal disease; workup should include bone marrow evaluation in atypical presentations. This aligns with the MDS-Treg data published this week in the malignant report.

## VII. Thalassemia (Alpha & Beta; Taiwan-Relevant)

**CRISPR-Cas12a genome-wide screen reveals PTPA phosphatase pathway in fetal hemoglobin silencing.**
Traxler E et al. *Blood* 2026-04-23. https://doi.org/10.1182/blood.2025030137

Two-tiered genetic screen (Cas12a genome-wide + Cas9 domain-focused) of all protein-coding genes for HBG1/2 regulation. Top new hit: **PTPA (PP2A phosphatase activator)**, whose loss elevates HBG1/2 while preserving erythroid differentiation. Mechanism: PTPA silences HBG1/2 primarily by regulating BCL11A expression. This is the most comprehensive CRISPR dissection of fetal hemoglobin regulation to date and identifies PTPA as a new pharmacologic target for hemoglobin-switching strategies in sickle cell disease and thalassemia.

**Taiwan context**: Alpha-thalassemia carrier rate ~4–5% in Taiwan; beta-thalassemia carriers ~1–2%. CRISPR-based HbF reactivation strategies (Casgevy/exa-cel approved in US/EU) are not yet covered by NHI Taiwan; the PTPA target may offer a pharmacologic (non-gene-editing) alternative.

## IX. Red Cell Disorders (G6PD, PK Deficiency, Hereditary Spherocytosis)

**Global burden of sickle cell disease in under-5 and under-20 — systematic review and meta-analysis.**
Pimenta K et al. *BJH* 2026-05-03. https://doi.org/10.1111/bjh.70501

Systematic review and meta-analysis; 57 studies, >3.6 million individuals, 56,593 recorded deaths (2017–2023).

| Age Group | Pooled Prevalence | Cause-specific Mortality Proportion |
|---|---|---|
| Under-5 | 0.008 (95% CI 0.004–0.016) | 0.021 |
| Under-20 | 0.009 (95% CI 0.005–0.017) | 0.029 (95% CI 0.001–0.621) |

Extreme heterogeneity and incomplete geographic coverage limit precision; data predominantly from sub-Saharan Africa. Underscores urgency for improved neonatal SCD screening, surveillance, and cause-of-death attribution globally.

**Erythropoietin elevation independently associates with thromboembolism in SCD.**
Zhang X et al. *BJH* 2026-04-29. https://doi.org/10.1111/bjh.70517

Combined analysis of UIC cohort and Walk-PHaSST study (N=557). Higher serum EPO concentration associated with increased thromboembolic risk (**combined OR 1.9, P=0.0029**). HbF% and Hb concentration correlated inversely with EPO. A genetic variant in EPOR (rs322139, G allele) associated with lower EPOR expression and lower EPO concentration in SCD (β = −0.23, P=6.4×10⁻⁵), trending toward lower thromboembolism (combined OR 0.64, P=0.054). This identifies EPO as a mediator of thrombotic risk in SCD beyond its role in erythropoiesis.

## XI. Thrombosis & Coagulation (VTE, APS, Inherited Thrombophilia)

**siRNA-mediated PAI-1 knockdown reduces thrombosis and prolongs lifespan in murine models.**
Ferraresso F et al. *Blood* 2026-04-30. https://doi.org/10.1182/blood.2025029834

Therapeutic siRNA-lipid nanoparticles (siPAI-1): one dose achieved **90% knockdown of plasma PAI-1 lasting 10 days** with no overt toxicity. In young and aged mice: decreased thrombus weight after IVC ligation; increased 4-day survival in aged mice post-IVC ligation. In obese mice: normalized supraphysiologic PAI-1. In fast-aging mouse models: extended lifespan. Hepatic PAI-1 mRNA correlated exponentially with body weight in obese mice; siPAI-1 achieved 70% knockdown. Represents a novel long-duration antithrombotic approach particularly relevant for age-related and obesity-related thrombotic risk.

**Anticoagulation, recurrent thrombosis, and major bleeding in APS.**
*Blood Advances* 2026 (recent). https://doi.org/10.1182/bloodadvances.2025019447

Analysis of anticoagulation outcomes in antiphospholipid syndrome, including recurrent thrombosis and major bleeding event rates. Relevant to ongoing debate over optimal anticoagulant choice (warfarin vs DOACs) in APS. Full abstract not captured.

**Cancer-associated VTE — reduced-dose vs full-dose apixaban: real-world comparison.**
Tan J et al. *BJH* 2026-04-28. https://doi.org/10.1111/bjh.70513

Comparison of extended reduced-dose apixaban (2.5 mg BID) vs standard full-dose (5 mg BID) in cancer-associated VTE management. Real-world safety and efficacy comparison. Abstract digest not available; clinically important for long-term anticoagulation decisions in cancer patients.

**VTE following surgery for pancreatic neuroendocrine neoplasms — RPTH commentary.**
*RPTH* 2026 (recent). https://doi.org/10.1016/j.rpth.2026.106622

Commentary on VTE risk following panNET surgery. Informs perioperative anticoagulation protocols.

**ETV6-related thrombocytopenia — cytoskeletal and granule defects underlie thrombus dysfunction.**
Tesakov I et al. *BJH* 2026-04-28. https://doi.org/10.1111/bjh.70495

12 patients from 6 families with germline ETV6 variants. Electron microscopy: spheroid platelet morphology, reduced alpha-granule pools, impaired shape maintenance. Proteomics: reduced alpha-granule and lysosomal proteins, imbalanced actin remodeling regulators, and β-tubulin disruption. Flow cytometry: preserved calcium signaling but impaired activation-dependent shape change, dense-granule release, and integrin activation. Ex vivo thrombus formation reduced independent of platelet count. Broader thromboinflammatory defect suggested by impaired granulocyte recruitment. Mechanistic insight for management of this rare inherited platelet disorder.

**Pathogenic PF4/polyanion antibodies — AJH update.**
*AJH* 2026 (recent). https://doi.org/10.1002/ajh.70333

Update on pathogenic PF4/polyanion antibodies — relevant to heparin-induced thrombocytopenia (HIT) and vaccine-induced immune thrombocytopenia/thrombosis (VITT). Abstract not available.

## XIII. Taiwan Clinical Context

- **ITP**: Eltrombopag and romiplostim are NHI-covered for refractory ITP in Taiwan; fostamatinib is not yet available. The CDK8/CDK19 and sex-stratified omics data are not yet practice-changing but suggest future precision medicine approaches.
- **PNH**: Eculizumab is NHI-covered (criteria: LDH ≥1.5× ULN, PNH clone ≥10%); ravulizumab and iptacopan/pegcetacoplan are not yet reimbursed. The iptacopan vs pegcetacoplan ITC data will be informative for future NHI applications.
- **Thalassemia**: Alpha-thalassemia carrier rate ~4–5%, beta ~1–2% in Taiwan. Blood transfusion support and chelation (deferasirox) are well-established. HbF-reactivating agents (luspatercept approved for MDS/beta-thalassemia) are available through NHI for transfusion-dependent beta-thalassemia. CRISPR approaches not yet locally available.
- **Aplastic anemia**: Horse ATG remains the preferred immunosuppressive regimen for pediatric patients at NTUH and CGMH; eltrombopag is combined with hATG as standard of care since the RACE trial.
- **Hemophilia**: Emicizumab is NHI-covered for HA with inhibitors; coverage for non-inhibitor HA is under review. Mim8 is not yet available. Extended half-life factors are available for HB.
- **VTE/APS**: DOACs (rivaroxaban, apixaban, edoxaban) are NHI-covered for treatment of VTE; warfarin remains preferred in Taiwan NHI guidelines for APS (triple-positive or arterial thrombosis). The reduced-dose apixaban cancer-VTE data are clinically relevant for local oncology practice.
- **G6PD**: G6PD deficiency prevalence in Taiwan is ~3–5% (males); relevant for drug prescriptions — avoid rasburicase, dapsone, nitrofurantoin, primaquine.

## XIV. Key Takeaways

1. CDK8/CDK19 inhibition is a mechanistically compelling new approach to ITP, converting effector T cells to Tregs via STAT5 signaling — watch for Phase 1 trials.
2. Sex-stratified omics at ITP diagnosis may predict chronicity with greater accuracy than clinical features alone.
3. In PNH, 2-year iptacopan data support durable efficacy; indirect comparisons with pegcetacoplan are informative for treatment choice but formal head-to-head data are lacking.
4. Horse ATG retains a definite role in very young children with immune-mediated aplastic anemia.
5. PAI-1 siRNA knockdown is a novel antithrombotic strategy with promising preclinical data, particularly in aging and obesity contexts.
6. The PTPA/BCL11A/HBG1/2 axis identifies new pharmacological targets for HbF reactivation that may eventually complement gene therapy approaches in thalassemia and SCD.
